Culture & Traditions

Turkmen Carpets

Turkmen carpets are renowned worldwide for their intricate patterns and exceptional craftsmanship. Traditionally handwoven from high-quality wool, these carpets reflect techniques passed down through generations.

Turkmen Cuisine

Traditional dishes such as plov and shashlik play a central role in Turkmenistan’s culinary heritage. Plov is a rich rice dish prepared with meat, vegetables, and aromatic spices, commonly served during celebrations and family gatherings.

Turkmen Festivals

Colorful celebrations in Turkmenistan are a vibrant expression of national identity and cultural pride. During national holidays, communities gather to enjoy traditional music, lively dances, and festive performances.Traditional attire is often showcased, reflecting its rich heritage.

Must-Visit Places

Darvaza Gas Crater

Known as the “Door to Hell,” this fiery crater has been burning for decades and is a spectacular sight at night.

Ashgabat

The capital city is famous for its white marble buildings, modern architecture, and cultural landmarks.

Kunya-Urgench

An ancient city with stunning mausoleums and historical monuments, reflecting Turkmenistan’s rich past.

Merv

One of the oldest Silk Road cities, featuring archaeological sites that span thousands of years of history.

Yangykala Canyon

A breathtaking natural canyon known for its vibrant colors and dramatic cliffs, perfect for photography and hiking.
